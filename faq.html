General 4 Questions SEC-06
► Q.24 What is technical analysis?
Technical analysis is the study of price movements and chart patterns to forecast future market direction. Unlike fundamental analysis, which looks at a company's financials, technical analysis focuses on what the price is doing — using historical price data, volume, candlestick patterns, support & resistance levels, trendlines, and technical indicators to identify trading opportunities. It is based on the principle that all information is already reflected in the price, and that price movements tend to follow recognizable patterns and trends.
► Q.25 Is stock market trading risky?
Yes, trading and investing in the stock market involves substantial risk of loss, and past performance is not indicative of future results. However, the purpose of education is to help you understand and manage that risk effectively. At Market Credo, we teach risk management, position sizing, stop-loss discipline, and capital preservation as integral parts of the curriculum. The goal is not to eliminate risk — which is impossible — but to make informed, educated decisions rather than emotional ones. Always invest only what you can afford to lose and consult a SEBI-registered advisor for investment decisions.
► Q.26 How is technical analysis different from fundamental analysis?
Fundamental analysis evaluates a company's intrinsic value by studying its financial statements, earnings, revenue, management, industry position, and economic factors. It answers the question: "Is this company worth buying?"

Technical analysis, on the other hand, studies price charts and market data. It focuses on patterns, trends, volume, and momentum to determine the best time to buy or sell. It answers the question: "When should I buy or sell?"

Both approaches have their merits. Many successful traders use a combination. At Market Credo, we specialize in technical analysis because we believe understanding price action is the most powerful skill a trader can develop.
